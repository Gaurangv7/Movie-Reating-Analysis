1. Business Problem

Film studios invest millions into production, but financial success and critical acclaim (IMDb ratings) do not always correlate. The goal of this project is to analyze a dataset of movies to identify which genres yield the highest Return on Investment (ROI) and how budget impacts critical reception.

2. Methodology

  • Step 1: Data Extraction (Python): A Python script was developed to simulate a dataset or connect to an API, cleaning the raw data and calculating derived metrics like Profit and ROI %.
  • Step 2: Transformation: Data was processed using the Pandas library to categorize movies into "Masterpiece", "Excellent", or "Good" based on rating thresholds.
  • Step 3: Visualization (Power BI): The processed CSV was imported into Power BI to create an interactive dashboard allowing for genre-based filtering.

3. Key Insights & Results

  • Genre Performance: Sci-Fi movies had the highest average budget but also the highest Box Office returns.
  • Rating Correlation: There is no direct linear correlation between high budgets and high IMDb ratings; some lower-budget Drama films scored higher than blockbusters.
  • Profitability: Action and Adventure movies showed the most consistent ROI, while Animation proved to be a safe investment with high floor ratings.
